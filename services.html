What I Offer

Software Requirement Elicitation

During my time as a Business Analyst, the Software Requirement Elicitation process was a core part of my job. In order to do this process effectively I conducted different brainstorming sessions, multiple stakeholder meetings and also I have experience on Brown paper sessions.

Requirements Analysis

This task was one of the most challenging tasks, I have faced in my career as a Business Analyst. In order to overcome this challenge I worked closely with Program Managers & Developers to make sure that all the business requirements are well documented, traceble and actionable.

Software Documentation

I have gained experience of multiple bid submissions and I'm highly skilled at writing technical reports and user manuals to benefit the end users. Apart from that, I have written countless Software Requirement specifications, UAT Sign off Documents and Functional specification documents.

Risk and Issue Management

I have gained the exposure on publishing project status reports and revising risk plans that can be happen in a major change of project schedule. Furthermore, I have also maintained Change Request logs, Incident logs and Project Governanace plans to make sure that all the issues are identified before the project is deployed.

Requirements Communication

As a Business Analyst with an IT background I know how important it is for developers to identify the business requiremnets effectively. Therefore, after the requirement elicitation process is completed, I have made process maps with help of Program Managers and Solutions Consultants and conducted demo sessions with Developers and QA Enginners to make sure that requirements are well identified.

Quality Assurance

I have written Test cases for mutiple software products. Therefore, I posesses the capability to create detailed, comprehensive and well structured test plans and test cases. I have also done both Manual and automation test case execution along with Database Verification, Defect Verification and User Acceptable Testing.
